Watch the A* pathfinding algorithm execute in real-time. It explores the terrain grid, evaluates cost functions based on slope hazards (red), and determines the optimal path to the doubly shadowed crater.

  • G
    G-Cost (Distance Travelled)
    Exact distance from the illuminated landing site start node, accounting for hazard penalties.
  • H
    H-Cost (Manhattan Heuristic)
    Estimated straight-line distance to the target crater, guiding the search efficiently.
  • F
    F-Cost (Total Priority)
    F = G + H + Hazard Penalty × 10. The node with the lowest F-cost is always explored next.
  • DRL
    Deep Reinforcement Learning
    A Deep Q-Network (DQN) overlay teaches the rover to dynamically balance battery drain vs. slope risk in unknown terrain.
